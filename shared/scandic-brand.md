# Scandic Brand Guidelines

## Brand Promise

**"The most comfortable stay"** — Scandic acts as "your friend in town," ensuring every guest feels valued and at ease. This promise extends to physical spaces, digital products, and every interaction.

**Core value: "Everyone is welcome"** — Inclusivity is non-negotiable. Every design decision must reinforce that Scandic is welcoming to absolutely everyone — families, solo travelers, business guests, all backgrounds.

## Brand Values

### Warmth
- Scandic is welcoming, friendly, and approachable
- Our digital products should feel like a trusted friend, not a polished advertisement
- Use warm, inviting language — not cold, transactional copy
- Design with empathy for every user's situation

### Scandinavian Simplicity
- Clean, functional, uncluttered design
- Every element should earn its place — remove before adding
- White space is a feature, not empty space — embrace the Nordic love of breathing room
- Straightforward interactions — don't make users think

### Authenticity Over Perfection
- Embrace analog warmth, real life, and even imperfection
- A perfectly composed image that feels nothing is less valuable than an imperfect one that feels real
- Design decisions should favor emotional resonance over visual polish
- Grainy textures, candid moments, and unstaged compositions are welcome when they convey genuine human connection

### Sustainability
- Consider the environmental footprint of design decisions
- Lighter pages = less energy = less carbon
- Optimize performance: fewer assets, smaller bundles, efficient code
- Design for longevity — avoid trendy patterns that age quickly

### Accessibility
- Not a compliance checkbox — it's core to our brand
- Scandic has a 100+ point accessibility standard — the most comprehensive in the hotel industry
- Digital must match our physical accessibility standards
- Design for permanent, temporary, and situational disabilities

## Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Friendly** but not casual — "your friend in town"
- **Helpful** but not patronizing
- **Clear** but not oversimplified
- **Confident** but not arrogant
- **Warm** and personal — every piece of copy should feel like helpful advice from someone who knows the city

### Writing Principles
- Lead with the benefit to the user
- Use plain language — avoid jargon and marketing-speak
- Be specific and actionable: "Book your stay" not "Reserve accommodation"
- Short sentences, short paragraphs
- Inclusive language always (avoid gendered terms, ableist language, cultural assumptions)
- Ground abstract promises in specific, sensory details ("your bed is ready, your coffee is hot, your room is quiet")

### CTAs
- Action-oriented, short: "Explore," "Book now," "Learn more," "Join Scandic Friends"
- Adapt tone to travel occasion: efficiency for business, warmth for family, indulgence for weekend getaways

## Color Palette

|---|---|---|
| Scandic Signal Red | `#cd0921` | Primary brand color — CTAs, primary buttons, key highlights, brand moments |
| Scandic Vivid Red | `#e30613` | Secondary red — hover states, badges, urgent/promotional elements |
| Nordic Trust Blue | `#004a99` | Secondary brand color — secondary buttons, links, informational, trust signals |
| Pure White | `#ffffff` | Text on colored backgrounds, primary page background |
| Soft Nordic Gray | `#f4f4f4` | Surface and card backgrounds, section dividers |
| Deep Charcoal | `#1a1a1a` | Primary body text — near-black for readability while softer than pure black |
| Warm Medium Gray | `#556068` | Secondary text, captions, metadata — meets 4.5:1 contrast on white |

### Color Philosophy
The palette is deliberately restrained — a Nordic approach where less is more. **Red leads, blue supports.** Avoid using red and blue in equal measure. Colors should frame authentic content, not compete with it — the palette recedes so that real imagery and human moments take center stage.

## Typography

**Font family:** Fira Sans (with system-ui, Avenir, Helvetica, Arial as fallbacks).

Fira Sans is a humanist sans-serif that balances geometric precision with warmth — aligned with Scandic's Nordic-modern personality.

| Role | Weight | Size | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero headings | Bold (700) | 36–48px | Commanding but welcoming. Short, punchy statements. |
| Section headings | Semi-Bold (600) | 24–32px | Clear wayfinding. Establishes content hierarchy. |
| Subheadings | Medium (500) | 18–20px | Supports main headings without competing. |
| Body text | Regular (400) | 16px | Comfortable reading. Line-height 1.5. |
| Captions & metadata | Regular (400) | 14px | Subtle secondary information. Uses Warm Medium Gray. |

- **Letter-spacing:** `0.012em` for buttons and UI labels
- **Line height:** 1.5 for body text, 1.2–1.3 for headings
- **Maximum line length:** 65–75 characters

## Imagery & Photography

The brand embraces **authenticity over perfection** — real life, analog warmth, and genuine human connection.

- **Emotional micro-moments over product shots:** Focus on the *feeling* — cold water on skin, sinking into comfortable sheets, the first sip of morning coffee, a child's laughter in a hotel corridor
- **Analog warmth and imperfection:** Grainy, slightly blurry, or unstaged photos are encouraged when they convey genuine warmth and connection. Imperfection signals authenticity
- **Real people, not models:** Both guests and staff depicted as real, authentic individuals. No staged poses, no stock-photo smiles. Capture people in actual moments
- **Warm, natural lighting:** Golden-hour warmth, natural daylight, cozy ambient lighting. No harsh flash or clinical setups
- **Nordic context, not postcard views:** Show the texture of Nordic life — rain on a window, birch trees, worn wooden floors — not just famous landmarks
- **Inclusive representation:** Every image set must reflect the full range of Scandic's guests — families, solo travelers, couples, business guests, all ages, all backgrounds
- **Full-bleed and immersive:** Images are large and set emotional context before the user reads a word

## Brand Strategy Context

### Travel Occasions Model
Scandic designs for **travel occasions**, not demographic personas. Digital experiences should adapt tone and emphasis to these occasions:

- **Pragmatic business travel** — efficiency, reliability, seamless booking
- **Family getaways** — reassurance, space, welcome for children
- **Romantic/indulgent weekends** — comfort, atmosphere, sensory delight
- **Group/meeting travel** — coordination, flexibility, professionalism

### Brand Carriers
Brand carriers connect abstract values to tangible guest experiences: "a warm welcome," "a good night's sleep," "a great breakfast." When building UI, ask: *which brand carrier does this touchpoint serve?*

Don't say "we care about comfort" — say "your bed is ready, your coffee is hot, your room is quiet." Ground abstract promises in specific, sensory details.

### Research Foundation
Qualitative and quantitative research (GEMIC) revealed that Scandic is perceived as highly functional, reliable, and accessible — but lacks emotional resonance. Customers view the brand as a safe "pain reliever" option. Design must close this emotional gap while preserving core strengths.

## Nordic Market Context

- Scandic operates across Sweden, Norway, Finland, Denmark, and other Northern European markets
- Consider localization needs (date formats, currency, language)
- Respect cultural nuances across Nordic countries
- High digital literacy audience with strong accessibility expectations
