# Role: Product Discovery & Research

Use this configuration if your primary role involves user research, product discovery, and evidence-based design decisions.

## Focus Areas

- User interviews and usability testing
- Research planning and synthesis
- Competitive and market analysis
- Journey mapping and service design
- Hypothesis framing and experiment design
- Insight documentation and sharing

## Key Responsibilities

### Research Planning
- Define research questions and objectives
- Choose appropriate methods (qualitative, quantitative, or mixed)
- Plan recruitment criteria reflecting diverse user groups
- Set up research repositories and templates

### Conducting Research
- User interviews: prepare guides, moderate sessions, capture notes
- Usability testing: define tasks, success criteria, and severity scales
- Surveys: design questions, avoid bias, analyze results
- Analytics review: identify patterns, define segments, track funnels

### Synthesis & Analysis
- Thematic analysis: code observations, identify patterns, build themes
- Affinity mapping: cluster insights into actionable groups
- Journey mapping: map user flows across touchpoints
- Persona development: evidence-based, not fictional

### Sharing Findings
- Research summaries with clear "so what" for each finding
- Insight cards: one insight per card, with evidence and confidence level
- Design implications: translate findings into specific design recommendations
- Present to stakeholders with appropriate level of detail

## Scandic Research Context

- Scandic uses a **travel occasions model** — research should be framed around occasions (pragmatic business, family getaways, romantic weekends, group travel) rather than demographic personas
- GEMIC research revealed Scandic is perceived as functional and reliable but lacks emotional resonance — research should explore closing this emotional gap
- Guests span business travelers, families, accessibility-dependent users, and event attendees
- Test across Nordic markets — cultural nuances matter
- Booking, check-in, and room experience are critical user journeys
- Accessibility research should include users with real disabilities, not just simulations
- Refer to `~/.claude/shared/DESIGN.md` Section 11 for full brand strategy context
